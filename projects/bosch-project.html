Bosch Gardening

Creating freedom whilst gardening

Look below

Background

Bosch representatives provided a brief which was open to interpretation. My group and I decided to develop a specialist product range that would focus on the gardening aspect of the company. My brief was to encourage gardening for those who are less advantaged and suffer from disabilities which limit their movement.

By researching the current market I noticed a market gap which I could explore. Delving into the rabbit hole I found that there was a significant lack of gardening products for disabled/unable users. Moreover, because of this discrepancy, it was actively and efficiently stopping people from gardening. There were visible signs that suggested that individuals who were over the age of 60+ slowly stopped gardening and with a vast drop in numbers at 70+.

Interviewing experts in the field and real users and making sure to get a wide array of abilities, I found my project aim. I was to develop a product that would effectively remove, turn-over, and burrow into the soil as this was deemed to be one of the harder tasks to perform by the interviewed gardeners.

User Testing

Vital research was carried out using different sketch models to determine which design was the most ergonomic. I also carried out this research with several users but kept one participant the same so they can provide insight into the progression of the product.

Next

Logo Ideation

I wanted to create a logo that would be instantly recognisable with or without the brand name next to it. The examples shown above are the two finalists. Adler would have been the stronger candidate, but the eagle carried too much meaning behind it because of past events. Therefore, Blühen was the desired choice as the logo and name were unique.

Next

Product Assembly

I produced CAD files on SolidWorks and produced the product prototype via the3D printing and CNC machines. The product colour scheme was determined as a group. I used orange paint for the components that the user would interact with.

Next

Product Review and Testing

I went back to the users and got them to assess the product. The conclusion was that the product assisted with their gardening and would encourage them to maintain their garden as it assisted in some of the harder tasks of gardening.

Next

Final Product

The project came with one functional and 1 aesthetic model. Above is the aesthetic model. This project received top remarks from the Bosch representative coming 3rd out of the entire year. My group received 1st place by both Bosch and internal assessors.
