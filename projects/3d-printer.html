Background

Currently, the issues faced in the manufacturing stages of prosthetic production are overwhelming. Per-year there are 1-million amputations performed globally with 91% of all amputations being lower limb. The USA spends $12-billion on aiding those who need/have an amputation. Moreover, waiting times are increasing and prosthetics artefacts are becoming more expensive to produce and purchase.

This project aimed at creating a manufacturing system to tackle the fundamental flaws faced with modern-day prosthetics. They are unchanged, uncomfortable, require excessive manufacturing time and skill, and overly expensive.

This manufacturing machine is estimated to save upwards of £5,000 (€5,500/$6,500) per prosthetic socket with further cost reductions made by the company as a result of not needing expensive staff. Furthermore, the material used for LAMLiquid Additive Manufacturing provides a multi-shore hardness product without the need to stop the print. The material is PURThermoset Polyurethane which when deposited chemically fuses to the layer below it, creating a solid structure. The end result by providing an artefact which provides substantial improvements over the other the competition.

Standalone Configuration

The Standalone unit is designed for ergonomic use either attached to a wall or connected to another unit. The above image displays how this dual-unit setup is configured. The product has been designed to be accessible for all sizes (within the 90th percentile). Moreover, this, compared to its competitors, takes up significantly less room and far fewer components making maintenance simpler to carry out.

Next

Table Top Configuration

The Table Top model was created to accommodate those who cannot give up the floor space for a Standalone unit. This product, like the Standalone, can either be attached to a wall or another unit. This 3D printer is the core of the machine. Inside the printer, the same components are used but its compact size comes with smaller material tanks.

Next

Product Internals

I designed the internals of both units to be very simplistic. The manufacturing of the Standalone or Table Top does not require different components which save on costs. Furthermore, making both models use the same components makes assembly and maintenance easier as technicians and mechanics only have to memorise one layout.

Next

ViscoTec Depositer

The material depositor of choice was a simple one, the use of the ViscoTec Vipro-HEAD 3/3 efficiently combines both catalyst and resin with controllable flow chambers which allow the material (developed by Chromatic3D) to provide a multi-shore hardness product.

Next

Standalone Frameset

Seen here, the Standalone Frameset (a.k.a SFSET), is comprised of the same structural components as the core unit (seen below). This provides an easier time with assembly as components such as the cross-struts can be assembled in mass and then brought to the main assembly area without the risk of components getting mixed up.

Next

Core Print Unit

Here we see what the internals of the print area (referred to as the ‘Core’) look like. On the Left, we see the back of the Core where there are cable guides and access to the servos. The electrical assembly (servos) are mounted upon a sliding tray which can be pulled out when maintenance is required. Moreover, the cable guides are snap closed, making it easy to remove or add cables and material transport tubes.
